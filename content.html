Grand Underground

In Pokémon Brilliant Diamond and Shining Pearl, the Grand Underground is a special area made up of a series of tunnels located beneath the Sinnoh region.

It must first be unlocked before a player can access the area. This cam be done once you reach Eterna City. Enter the house to the right of the Pokémon Center and the speak to the old man inside, he will give you the Explorer Kit, a Key Item that allows access to the Grand Underground from anywhere as long as you are outside in the open. Upon use of the Explorer Kit you will end up at the corresponding location in the Grand Underground compared to your location in the overworld.

The Grand Underground is larger than its generation 4 counter-part, and corresponds with the entire overworld map of Sinnoh.

While in the Underground you can encounter walls that will sparkle that can be dug up by the player. Interacting with such a wall initiates a mini-game, where can be rewarded with rare items upon completion. Some of these items are only available from this source.

Also present in the Underground are Pokémon Hideaways are new places to encounter Wild Pokémon, some of the Pokémon can only be found in Hideaways. All Pokémon found in these new areas will have at least one Egg Move if applicable. The strength and variety of Pokémon found in Hideaways will increase as you make progress on your journey to becoming the Champion of the Pokémon League. Each of the six areas found in the Underground will contain at least one large Hideaway.

Ramanas Park

While the Underground can be accessed pretty early on in your journey in the Sinnoh region Ramanas Park is post-game content, and can only be accessed after you have successfully conquered the Pokémon League and become the new Champion as well as complete the Sinnoh region Pokédex. Once you have fulfilled both these prerequisites, head back to Sandgem Town and talk to Professor Rowan, after talking to Rowan Professor Oak will make an appearance and suggest you visit Ramanas Park.

This area is where it possible to catch Legendary Pokémon not usually present in the Sinnoh region, these are outlined on the first page, or you click HERE! to go there now. There are 2 more Pokémon that can be found in the Park not mentioned on the first, and thats because they can be found no matter the version of the game. They are Mewtwo and Rayquaza.

In order to summon Legendary Pokémon in the Park you will need to acquire their respective Slate, these are gotten by gotten by trading with a vendor who is present in the Park. The range of Slates available for purchase will increase as you catch more Legendary Pokémon.

The Slates for each Legendary Pokémon are as follows:

Johto Slate Raikou Entei Suicune
Soul Slate Latios Latias
Rainbow Slate Ho-oh
Squall Slate Lugia
Oceanic Slate Kyogre
Tectonic Slate Groudon
Stratospheric Slate Rayquaza
Genome Slate Mewtwo

The Great Marsh

The Great Marsh is located in Pastoria City, it has an entry cost of 500 pokédollars, this gives you 30 Safari Balls and a limited number of steps (500) in the area to catch Pokémon. Your time in the area will come to an end when one of the afore mentioned runs out. Each day different Pokémon appear inside the Marsh.

The Great Marsh is also home to lots of bogs, which if stepped in will cause the player to sink into the mud, to get out of bog you need to move around the analog sticks on the switch but this does use up your valuable steps inside the Marsh. It is best to do what you can to avoid stepping in bogs.

If you are still in the building before you enter the Marsh then you can go to the second floor and use the provided binoculars to scout the areas in the Marsh, this is extremely helpful when you are looking for specific Pokémon.

Super Contest Show

The Super Contest Show is located in Hearthome City, can can be accessed when you first arrive in Hearthome. Inside the building you will be able to enter your Pokémon into contests and they will compete based suits them best. There's Coolness, Cuteness, Beauty, Cleverness, and Toughness. You will start out the lowest possible rank Normal, the higher ranks are Great, Ultra, and Master. As you may have guess they scale in the same order as Poké balls.

Any stickers you have earned by beating Gym Leaders will come in use for the Super Contest Show as having a Ball Capsule full of stickers will increase your Pokémon's score when it comes to judging.
