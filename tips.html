Tips, Tricks, and Helpful Info

Seeing as Brilliant Diamond and Shining Pearl are remakes of games from well over a decade ago they don't follow the modern design of the recent Pokémon games (Let's Go, Pikachu! and Let's Go, Eevee!,Sword and Shield), while some quality of life changes have been made to make the experience better, something's have remained the same. Although the things that have remained the same are to be expected really seeing as they are areas/zones of the game and not something as simple as User Interface tweak to make navigating Sinnoh easier.

Type Advantages

Like all games in the series, type-advantages play a major part in how easy your journey in the Sinnoh region will be. This is made easier now with the addition of text under each of your Pokémon's abilities stating their effectiveness, however this text is only present the second time you encounter that specific Pokémon, the first time you face the Pokémon you will need to either consult an external tool to get its type and moves are effective against it or you may just have this knowledge already from playing previous games. If you want an external tool to keep open and help with this I suggest this Type Calculator.

Mystery Gifts

Mystery Gifts are gifts given out sometimes by the Pokémon Company as rewards for players. Once you have acquired your first gym badges you unlock the ability to receive gifts. The Mystery Gift menu can be accessed by pressing the "X" button on the switch and then selecting the bottom right option.

There are various things that can be given out through Mystery Gifts, most frequently things such as Poké Balls will be given out and sometimes Pokémon will be given out and even rarer than that is when Shiny Pokémon are given to players through Mystery Gifts.

Useful TM's, You May Have Missed

There are a couple TM's (formally HMs) that are needed to traverse the Sinnoh region and while you will naturally come across most of the ones you need you could easily miss out a few key ones.

Defog

You're given Defog after talking to a NPC just inside the entrance of The Great Marsh, just pay the entrance fee for area and talk to the NPC to get the TM.

Fly

Fly is located just inside the entrance of the Team Galactic warehouse in Veilstone City, just walk in and take the Poké Ball located between the two storage containers.

Flash

Flash can be purchased for 1000 Poké Dollars on the 3rd floor of the Veilstone Department Store from the NPC located in the upper left area of the floor. More of a niche TM but my be useful.

Waterfall

After obtaining the eight gym badge in Sunnyshore City, head to the north most point of the city, there a NPC named Jasmine will approach you and give you the TM for Waterfall.

Poké Balls

They are many different kind of Poké Balls present in the game, each with its own effect. Using the correct ball for the given scenario will potentially save you from losing out a Pokémon you were trying to catch but got away before you could successfully catch it.

Each Pokémon will have a different Catch Rate when compared to another so using the correct Poké Ball for the situation can be crucial for catching the Pokémon before it faints or runs away.

Item Effect Catch Rate
Poké Ball Standard Poké Ball. (1x)
Great Ball Provides a higher catch rate than regular Poké Ball. (1.5x)
Ultra Ball Provides a higher catch rate than a Great Ball. (2x)
Master Ball 100% success chance when used. (100%)
Luxury Ball Wild Pokémon will quickly grow friendly after being caught. (1x)
Dive Ball Works especially well on Pokémon that live underwater. (3.5x)
Dusk Ball Makes catching Pokémon at night or in caves easier. (3.5x)
Heal Ball Heals and removes any status effects when used to catch a Pokémon. (1x)
Nest Ball Becomes more effective the lower the level of the Pokémon. (Up to 4x)
Net Ball More effective when used on Water or Bug type Pokémon. (3.5x)
Premier Ball Commemorative item, used just for its appearance. (1x)
Quick Ball Increases catch chance if used at the start of an encounter. (4x)
Repeat Ball More effective when used on a Pokémon species that has been caught before. (3.5x)
Safari Ball Used in the Great Marsh. (1.5x)
Timer Ball Progressively more effective as more turns are made. (Up to 4x)
