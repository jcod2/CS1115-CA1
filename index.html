Introduction

Welcome to my Quick-Start guide and comparison for Pokémon Brilliant Diamond and Shining Pearl. Released on the 19th of November 2021 for the Nintendo Switch, these releases are remakes of the Generation IV games Diamond and Pearl, originally released on the 27th of July 2007 (Europe) for the Nintendo DS.

Changes from the Originals

Unlike their originals, these new versions feature a new area called Ramanas Park an area which will let the player catch Legendary Pokémon from the previous games, it is only accessible after reaching the post game (after beating the elite four). Each game will have its own pool of Legendary Pokémon for the player to catch from.

Many quality of life updates have been made seeing as the this point the original games have been out for nearly 15 years, and since then the games themselves have evolved with each new release. HMs have been more or less removed, now instead of needing to teach it your Pokémon with the requirements needed, now you use HMs from an app on your Pokétech. Any of the HMs present in the original games have now been made into TMs that you can teach your Pokémon for use in battle.

The Underground has been expanded into the Grand Underground, it now has six areas compared to the originals games four. Players will now be able to encounter Pokémon in the grand underground, areas known as Pokémon Hideaways are now present and have different natural biomes, some Pokémon will only be available through these areas. Even Pokémon not normally found in the Sinnoh region can now be found in these Hideaways.

Starter Pokémon

The three starter Pokémon for the Sinnoh region are Turtwig, Chimchar and Piplup. They are Grass, Fire and Water type respectively. While you will be fine with any stater, there advantages and disadvantages for each one. Turtwig is probably the best early option for the easiest start to your journey throughout Sinnoh, while Chimchar is likely to tbe the best in the long term due to games lackluster choice of Fire-Type Pokémon throughout the region. There are a lot of options when it comes to Water-Type Pokémon so unless you are particularly drawn to Piplup one of the other two starters will be a better option to get started in Sinnoh.

At the end of the day your starter choice will have little impact on how you progress along on your journey as you will encounter countless other Pokémon that you may prefer because they are stronger or simply because you prefer how they look. Its a Pokémon game, it can be beaten with any combination of Pokémon if your willing to grind out exp.

Pokétech Apps

Like in other games in the series there is a "smart watch" like device available for the player to use. There a total of 20 Apps available to the player, each with its own function. The Pokétech is acquired in Jubilife City, you get it after talking with the all the Clowns present around the city and correctly answering their questions. You'll acquire the other apps as you progress on your journey, the Pokétech is accessed in-game by pressing the R button on your controller, you can cycle through the apps on your Pokétech by pressing in the Right Stick.
